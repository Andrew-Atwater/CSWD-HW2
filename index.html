About LeBron James

LeBron James is an NBA player for the Los Angeles Lakers, and is arguably the most famous basketball player of all time. Born in 1984, the Akron, Ohio native has been dominating the game since being drafted in 2003 by the Cleveland Cavaliers, his hometown team. Over 23 years, James has averaged 27 points, 7 rebounds, and 7 assists, a consistent superstar-level performance that has earned him an NBA record 22 All-Star appearances. James played for the Cavaliers from 2003-2010, took his talents to South Beach with the Miami Heat for the 2010-2014 seasons, returned to Cleveland from 2014-2018, and finally signed with the Los Angeles Lakers where he still plays today.

Lebron's Accolade Summary

Alongside James' 22 All-Star Game appearances, he has also clocked numerous NBA records and awards. He is the all-time total points leader with 43,111 and counting, with 21 total All-NBA team awards, 6 total All-Defensive team awards, 4 MVP awards, and 4 NBA Championship wins also winning Finals MVP each time. Outside the NBA, he also has 3 Gold and 1 Bronze Olympic medals for his work with Team USA in Men's 5-on-5 Basketball in the Summer Olympics (Bronze 2004, Gold 2008, 2012, 2024). Because of these awards, his longevity and high skill level in the NBA, and the competition he has had to face, many consider James to be the greatest basketball player to ever live.
